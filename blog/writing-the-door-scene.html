Writing the Night the Door Opens

By Eric Brister · May 28, 2026 · 5 min read

The opening chapter of On the List went through eleven drafts. For ten of them, Mara walked through the door because she was brave. That was the lie I had to kill.

The version that didn't work

Bravery is boring at the start of a book. A character who chooses danger has already told you who she is. I wanted a character who is chosen by it — who finds her own name on a card she never filled out and has to live with the vertigo of that.

The line that fixed it

"She had not been let in. She had been called." Once I had that, the whole chapter reorganized itself around it. Everything before it is misdirection; everything after it is consequence.
